# Frequently Asked Questions

## General Questions

### What is FIRE?

FIRE (Florida Intensive Rope Events) is an educational organization that hosts three annual rope bondage conventions in Orlando, Florida. Each event is designed for different skill levels: BLAZE (beginner to intermediate), FLARE (intermediate to advanced), and FIRE (advanced to expert).

### Who can attend FIRE events?

FIRE events are open to adults 18 and older who are interested in rope bondage education. Whether you're a complete beginner or an experienced practitioner, we have an event designed for your skill level. All attendees must agree to our code of conduct and policies.

### Do I need experience to attend?

It depends on the event. BLAZE is designed for beginners and those building foundational skills, so no prior experience is required. FLARE and FIRE are geared toward intermediate to advanced practitioners and assume familiarity with basic rope techniques.

### What should I bring?

At minimum, bring rope appropriate for your skill level (typically 25-30 feet of 6mm jute or hemp per tie, with 4-6 lengths recommended). You may also want to bring water, snacks, a notebook, and any personal items you need for comfort. Specific packing lists are provided to registered attendees before each event.

### Can I attend alone or do I need a partner?

You can attend with or without a partner. Many attendees come solo and find practice partners at the event. We facilitate partner matching and encourage open communication about practice preferences and boundaries.

### Are FIRE events accessible?

The Woodshed, our primary venue, is a ground-floor space with accessible parking and restroom facilities. If you have specific accessibility needs, please contact us before the event so we can accommodate you.

### What is your photo policy?

Photography and video recording are strictly prohibited in class and practice spaces without explicit consent from all parties being photographed. Official event photographers may be present in designated areas. We take privacy seriously and enforce this policy rigorously.

## Policies & Code of Conduct

### What is your code of conduct?

All attendees must treat others with respect, practice enthusiastic consent, and maintain appropriate boundaries. Harassment, discrimination, non-consensual touching, and violations of event policies will result in immediate removal without refund. Our full code of conduct is provided during registration.

### What is your consent policy?

Consent is mandatory for all rope practice and any physical contact. "No" means no. "Maybe" means no. Only an enthusiastic "yes" means yes. Consent can be withdrawn at any time. We have a zero-tolerance policy for consent violations.

### Is alcohol or drug use permitted?

No. FIRE events are substance-free. Rope bondage requires clear-headedness, good judgment, and full control of your faculties. Attendees who appear to be under the influence will not be permitted to participate in classes or rope practice.

### What is your safer sex policy?

While rope itself is non-sexual, we acknowledge that rope can be intimate and sensual. Any sexual activity must be consensual, private, and conducted in accordance with venue rules. Public sexual activity is not permitted.

### Can I leave and re-enter the event?

Yes, registered attendees may leave and return during event hours. You'll receive a wristband or badge to facilitate re-entry.

## Venue & Location

### Where are FIRE events held?

Most FIRE events are held at The Woodshed, located at 6431 Milner Blvd Suite #4, Orlando, FL 32809. This is a private dungeon and event space designed for BDSM and rope education.

### Is there parking available?

Yes, The Woodshed has a private parking lot. Parking is free for event attendees.

### Are there hotels nearby?

Yes, there are numerous hotels within a 10-15 minute drive of the venue. We provide a list of recommended accommodations to registered attendees. Some hotels may offer group rates for FIRE attendees.

### Can I bring food?

Light snacks and non-alcoholic beverages are typically available at events. You're welcome to bring your own food and drinks. The venue has a designated eating area.

### Is the venue private?

Yes, The Woodshed is a private, members-only space. FIRE events are closed to the general public and only accessible to registered attendees.

## Tickets & Registration

### How do I register?

Tickets are sold through ForbiddenTickets.com. Links to purchase tickets are available on each event page on our website. Registration opens several weeks before each event.

### When do tickets go on sale?

Ticket sale dates are announced on our website and social media channels. Generally, tickets go on sale 6-8 weeks before each event. Early bird pricing is often available.

### What is your refund policy?

Refunds are available up to 30 days before the event, minus a processing fee. Within 30 days of the event, tickets are non-refundable but may be transferred to another person with prior approval. Contact us as soon as possible if you need to request a refund or transfer.

### Do you offer scholarships or discounts?

We occasionally offer scholarships or work-trade opportunities. Information about available scholarships is posted during ticket sales. Active military, veterans, and returning attendees may qualify for discounts. Contact us for details.

### Are tickets transferable?

Yes, tickets can be transferred to another eligible person with advance notice. Contact us with both the original ticket holder's information and the new attendee's information to process a transfer.

### What payment methods do you accept?

Tickets must be purchased through ForbiddenTickets.com, which accepts major credit cards and other electronic payment methods. We do not accept cash or checks for ticket purchases.

## Contact & Support

### How can I contact the FIRE team?

Email us at <EventInfo@FireOrlando.com>. We typically respond within 48 hours. For urgent matters during an event, contact information for on-site staff will be provided to registered attendees.

### Can I volunteer at FIRE events?

Yes! We rely on volunteers to help make our events successful. Volunteer opportunities include registration support, room monitoring, setup/cleanup, and more. Volunteers often receive discounted or complimentary admission. Contact us to learn more.

### How can I become a presenter?

We're always looking for skilled educators who align with our values. If you're interested in teaching at a FIRE event, send us an email with your teaching experience, rope background, and proposed class topics. Include links to your work if available.

### Can I vend at FIRE events?

We work with a limited number of vendors who offer rope, gear, and related products. If you're interested in vending, contact us with information about your business and products.
